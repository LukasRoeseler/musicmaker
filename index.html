How to use & keyboard shortcuts
  • Play notes: on-screen keys, your computer keyboard, or a connected MIDI keyboard. The computer-key mapping follows physical key positions, so it works the same on US-QWERTY and German-QWERTZ keyboards. White keys: A S D F G H J K L ; — black keys: W E T Y U O P.
  • Pads (drums): click pads or press number keys 1–8 (top row or numpad). MIDI drum channel 10 maps to pads.
  • Sounds: pick an instrument from the Sound menu — Grand Piano (default), Electric Piano, Organ, Accordion, Harmonica, Strings, Synth Lead.
  • Knobs: drag up/down or scroll. They drive the synth (and respond to MIDI CC 70-93). Vibrato = mod wheel (CC 1).
  • Sequencer tab: a mini piano roll — click cells to draw notes, drag a note's right edge to make it longer, click a note to remove it, and toggle the drum-step grid. Press Play to loop; Export MIDI to save.
  • Octave: the two bottom-left keys (Z / X on QWERTY, Y / X on QWERTZ) or the ◀/▶ Oct buttons.
  • Loop machine: Space = Play/Stop, R = Record. Arm Record then play — your performance loops. Add tracks to layer.
  • MIDI: connect a device (USB or Bluetooth). On phones use Chrome (Android) or a Web MIDI browser. Pick an Output + enable MIDI Thru to drive external gear.
