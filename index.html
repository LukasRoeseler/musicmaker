How to use & keyboard shortcuts
  • Play notes: on-screen keys, your computer keyboard, or a connected MIDI keyboard. The computer-key mapping follows physical key positions, so it works the same on US-QWERTY and German-QWERTZ keyboards. White keys: A S D F G H J K L ; ' (German: … L Ö Ä) — black keys: W E T Y U O P plus # (German).
  • Pads (drums): click pads or press number keys 1–8 (top row or numpad). MIDI drum channel 10 maps to pads.
  • Sounds: pick an instrument from the Sound menu — pianos, organ, accordion, harmonica, strings; guitars (Acoustic, Electric, Rock-distortion, Post-Rock with tremolo); and sci-fi/math-rock synths (Sci-Fi Pad, Hyperspace Lead, Warp Bass, Crystal Bells). Tip: add Delay + Reverb for the post-rock / 65daysofstatic vibe.
  • Drum kits: switch the pad sound with the Kit menu (Real Drums, Acoustic, 808, 909, Lo-Fi) — on both tabs.
  • Arpeggiator / repeat: turn on Arp Keys and hold notes to cycle them (Up, Down, Up/Down, Random, As Played, Chord) at the chosen rate; Repeat Pads does the same for held pads. Both follow the Loop Machine's BPM and can be recorded.
  • Loop machine (top): always visible above the tabs. Record anything you play (keys, pads, MIDI, even arpeggios). From the Sequencer use ⤴ Send to Loop to drop your pattern in as new tracks.
  • FX: Reverb and Delay are off by default (and never applied to drums) — dial them in with the knobs.
  • Themes: choose Dark, Light, Retro, or Futuristic from the Theme menu (remembered between visits).
  • Knobs: drag up/down or scroll. They drive the synth (and respond to MIDI CC 70-93). Vibrato = mod wheel (CC 1).
  • Sequencer tab: a mini piano roll — click cells to draw notes, drag a note's right edge to make it longer, click a note to remove it, and toggle the drum-step grid. Use 🎲 Randomize to generate a drum beat. Press Play to loop; Export MIDI to save.
  • Octave: the two bottom-left keys (Z / X on QWERTY, Y / X on QWERTZ) or the ◀/▶ Oct buttons.
  • Loop machine: Space = Play/Stop, R = Record. Arm Record then play — your performance loops. Add tracks to layer. Each track remembers the sound it was recorded with (shown as a badge), so changing the instrument later won't alter existing tracks — pick a new sound for each new track.
  • MIDI: connect a device (USB or Bluetooth). On phones use Chrome (Android) or a Web MIDI browser. Pick an Output + enable MIDI Thru to drive external gear.
